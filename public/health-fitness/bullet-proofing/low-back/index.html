Low Back

Thu, Apr 2, 2026

Good Mornings and Reverse Hyper are your big two, ask the front desk about reverse hyper machine
Then stability/abs
Then bridge just for safety/coolness (maybe morning routine) Morning routine - sun salutation, pidgeon/lizard, worldest greatest stretch, bridge (then bridge reach, until you can do a backbend+back hamstring)

Cut out bent over rows
GHD if they don’t have the Nordic curl machine (it’s like the same thing)

Bridge
Good Mornings
QL Raise
Should pres thing
RDLs
BRACE - this is your biggest safety net, work on stability (Hip airplane, lateral load)+ AB Rollouts to get this better - practice this hard when you workout (actively think about it)
⭐⭐⭐Reverse Hypers (done at the end of deadlift or leg day)
Mc Gill big 3/ Ab wheel+L-sit routine

  • For workouts/whatever you don’t wanna feel it in your low back, but in your hamstrings/but/abs/anything - your low back should never be the main mover and shouldn’t be felt obviously your spine can’t adapt like muscles, it’s bone, the musculature around the back exists to keep it stable

==Maybe rows should be chest supported== like high row/row row loaded machiens
Volume hip hinges + Volume squats (try it out)
Try out dynamic double progression

  • harder to do an additional rep than just add 2.5 and do the same though
  • I responded well to the pryamid kinda thing but also, like just starting at my 5x5 weight on bench and doing all 5 sets is overall better, but I got a lot better results on the row

Not back extension
Not Jefferson curl (basically anything that puts a curved back under load)

When you destroy your weakness a new one will arise, this is good though (my low back was the thing that was always the limiting factor in my recovery, but I’ve been working at it for so long that it’s now my hamstrings - how sweet would that be)
