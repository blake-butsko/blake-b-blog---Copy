STEP 4: THE PATRICK STEP

I believe the Patrick Step is the most likely necessary accessory exercise to the ATG Split Squat, which can be used as a regression for beginners, and still as an effective warm-up for even the most advanced.

Go for height (6-7" is the general max, two bumper plates) before weight - I feel like this is a regressed version of the pistol squat, but then this gets replaced by weighted slant board and atg split squat
In dense you do loaded reverse step ups which is the same as Patrick step

STEP 7: ELEPHANT WALK

This is the first of the “accessory” exercises in the Knee Ability Zero formula. Everything we have done to this point directly contributes to you being able to handle more force through your leg muscles, but you can only use the muscles that extend your knee (the quads) to the degree that the muscles which flex the knee (the hamstrings) can stretch

We will progress this until we’re hands on the floor, with our hands right in front of our toes

Look how far he extends his knee forward
You’re just gonna be walking your hands towards the feet

Two stretches

Progression for the hamstring is:
Elephant walk till feet, Load with Nordic Curl, and removing tightness behind the knees with the Jefferson curl, and prepping lower back with seated good morning, leading to the standard of 100% weight RDL with knees behind ankle & Lower back below parallel + Full range nordic with glute contraction - then eventually 100% BDW one leg RDL
Hip flexor is L-sit, weighted reverse squat, …
then further combination with the pancake stretch

The Workout

3 Times a week for 12 weeks - Optional added after week 1/2
Back pedals on treadmill/Deadmill (treadmill turned off so you push it yourself)/Backwards sled 10 minutes - 5 minutes with sled
1: Tibialis Raise: 25 reps -no break-
2: THE FHL Calf Raise -no break-
2B: Tibialis Raise: 25 reps -no break-
3: KOT Calf Raise 25 reps
3B (OPTIONAL): Nordic Curl 5 rounds of 1 minute (5 reps a minute) (make sure there’s padding under knees)
Step 4: Patrick Step: 25 reps - 1 set minimum but you can perform up to 3 sets per side - no breaks needed - if this is a weak point for you.
//Repeat
Step 5: ATG Split Squat: 5 sets of 5 reps per side with 30 second breaks between each.
Step 6: KOT Squat (OPTIONAL!): 5 sets of 5 reps with short breaks, no more than 30 seconds per set.
Step 7A: Slant Board Squat (Optional-week 2): 3 X 20
Step 7B: Cossacks Squat (Loaded) 5X5
//Repeat^
Step 7: Elephant Walk: 36 reps per side
Step 8: L-Sit: 60 seconds - 1 set minimum but you can perform up to 3 sets, with 1-2 minute breaks, if this is a weak point for you.
Step 9: Couch Stretch: 60 seconds per side - 1 set minimum but you can perform up to 3 sets if this is a weak point for you. (make sure there’s padding under knees)

  • Hamstring curl if not nordic curl (or with it), Knee raise (builds hip flexor)
    Feet, Ankle, Tibialis, Calf, Patellar Tendon, (KNEE), VMO, IT band/Piriformis, Quad/Hamstring, Flexor, Hip
